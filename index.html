"History doesn't repeat itself, but it often rhymes."

-Mark Twain

While the origins of the above quote are cloudy, the meaning is quite clear, and especially to sailors heading out to sea. In the context of wind direction and strength, the phenomena of autocorrelation applies: the conditions for today are highly likely to be similar to those of yesterday. If it was breezy yesterday, then it's likely to be breezy today. This can be enormously helpful to a sailor selecting a sail for the day...it can be just as helpful to anyone selecting a jacket for the day.
